How to play Nasty

Get all 5 of your tees from your base, once around the board clockwise, and into your home row before anyone else. Teams: you and the player straight across win together.

Getting out

  • King or Ace brings a tee out of your base and onto your starting block - the first hole of your lap.

Cards

  • Ace - come out, or move 1.
  • King - come out only.
  • Queen - move 12.
  • Jack - trade places with any other tee on the board.
  • 3 - move one of your tees 3 holes backwards (never behind your own starting block).
  • Everything else - move that many holes forward.

The nasty part

  • Land exactly on an opponent's tee and it's blasted back to base.
  • You can never pass your own tee - or your partner's, in teams - and you can never land on your own tee. Any play that would do either simply isn't allowed.
  • Landing on your partner's tee is a last resort - it's only allowed when it's the ONLY play you have left, and then their tee goes back to base. Sorry, partner!

Home row

  • Exact count only - if your card is too big, that tee can't use it.
  • A tee that's snug (pushed all the way up) is safe forever.
  • A tee that's not snug can still be forced back out by your own 3.

Turns

  • Cards get dealt in rounds. 4 players: 5, then 4, then 4 (all 52 cards used). 6 players: 4, then 4 (48 of 52 used - the leftover 4 sit unused in the dealer's pile until the deck passes to the next dealer and gets reshuffled). Play one card per turn.
  • No playable card? You're skipped automatically until the next deal.
  • If a partner finishes, they keep playing to move their partner's tees.

Online games work a little differently

  • No Skip button - everyone watches the same game at the same speed.
  • The host picks the game speed for the table.
  • Anyone can pause for everyone.
  • You can switch apps anytime. The game keeps going and catches you up when you return. If your board ever looks stuck, close and reopen the app.
  • When a player's name turns red, they are offline. The game keeps going and they catch up when they come back.
  • Need to leave for good? Have a computer take over your seat (under Pause/Save).
